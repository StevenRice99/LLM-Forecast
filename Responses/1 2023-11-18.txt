The articles discuss the recent surge in COVID-19 cases, hospitalizations, and deaths in Ontario's long-term care homes, leading to the reinstatement of mask mandates for staff. Here are some key points:
1. COVID-19 cases and hospitalizations : Between August 27 and October 28, 2023, there were 5,459 COVID-19 cases among LTC home residents, with 181 hospitalizations.
2. Masking mandate reinstated : In response to the surge in cases and outbreaks, the Ministry of Long-Term Care has reinstated a masking mandate for all staff, including students, support workers, and volunteers.
3. Respiratory virus outbreaks : There was a significant increase in respiratory virus outbreaks over September and October in long-term care homes.
4. Wastewater surveillance data : Wastewater surveillance data shows a slow uptick in the virus as of November 9, suggesting that cases may peak in the next few weeks.
5. High positivity rate : The overall positivity rate for COVID-19 in Ontario is high at 17%.
6. Impact on long-term care homes : The surge in COVID-19 cases has led to increased hospitalizations (181) and deaths (106) among residents due to COVID-19.
These articles highlight the ongoing challenges of managing COVID-19 outbreaks in long-term care homes, particularly during periods of high community transmission.
