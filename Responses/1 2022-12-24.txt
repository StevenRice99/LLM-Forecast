Here is a summary of each article:
 Article 1 
The article discusses the importance of transparency in reporting communicable disease cases to inform public health advice and encourage vaccination. It highlights a potential lag in reporting pediatric flu deaths in Ontario, which may be relevant for forecasting COVID-19 hospitalizations if similar delays occur.
 Article 2 
This article discusses the issue of a "two-tiered" system in Ontario's healthcare sector, where some workers are paid better and receive benefits like sick days, while others are low-wage and work on contract. Experts emphasize the importance of paid sick leave for healthcare workers, citing reports that over 27,000 Ontario workers had been infected with COVID-19 on the job.
 Article 3 
The article highlights a potential outbreak of invasive Group A Streptococcal (iGAS) infections in Montreal, which can cause serious illnesses such as strep throat and necrotizing fasciitis. It provides information on who is most at risk, symptoms to watch out for in children and adults, and how people can protect themselves and children.
 Article 4 
This article reports on overcrowding in New Brunswick hospitals, where patients are being placed in non-traditional care spaces such as supply rooms due to a shortage of nursing home beds. The wait list for nursing home beds has reached a record high of 833, with 483 seniors waiting for a bed.
Note that none of the articles mention Ontario specifically, except for Article 1 and Article 2 which discuss issues related to Ontario's healthcare sector.
