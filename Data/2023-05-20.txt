Title: Drop in cancer diagnoses in 2020 possibly linked to COVID-19 disruptions: Statistics Canada
Publisher: CBC News
Posted: 3 days ago
The pandemic-related disruptions in screening services led to a 12.3% decline in new cancer diagnoses in 2020.
This may result in a rise in cancer mortality rates as patients' conditions worsen due to delayed treatment.
The article highlights the "collateral damage" of COVID-19 on healthcare, including delayed or foregone cancer screenings and treatments.
The data show that throat and anal cancers had higher incidence rates in 2020 compared to the previous five-year average.
