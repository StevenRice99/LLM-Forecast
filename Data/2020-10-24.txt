Article 4 of 6
Title: Impacts of COVID-19 on immigrants and people designated as visible minorities
Publisher: Statistique Canada
Posted: 4 days ago
Here are five key points about immigrants and COVID-19:
1. Increased fear of harassment: Immigrants were almost twice as likely as Canadian-born individuals to report being afraid of unwanted behaviors (29% vs 17%), with a significant increase in fear of stigmatization due to racial identity (42% vs 9%).
2. Precautions and vaccine uptake: Immigrants were more concerned about health risks and took precautions, such as wearing masks (80% vs 61%) and avoiding crowds (93% vs 81%). However, they were less likely to get vaccinated (52% vs 59%).
3. Concerns about social issues: Immigrants reported higher concerns than Canadian-born individuals about various social issues, including violence in the home (11.6% vs 7%), family stress from confinement (38.2% vs 30.1%), and maintaining social ties (43.7% vs 30.1%).
4. Impact on immigration: The pandemic led to a significant decline in immigration flows, with only 34,260 immigrants entering Canada in the second quarter of 2020 compared to 94,280 in the same period in 2019.
5. Opportunities for skilled immigrants: Despite the challenges, accelerated digitalization after COVID-19 may create opportunities for skilled immigrants and visible minorities, who are more likely to have higher education qualifications (50% vs 28%) and STEM backgrounds (37% vs 18%).
