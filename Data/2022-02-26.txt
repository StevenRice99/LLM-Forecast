Article 4 of 6
Title: More evidence vaccines prevent ICU admission and mortality from SARS-CoV-2
Publisher: News-Medical.Net
Posted: 3 days ago
20,064 participants hospitalized with COVID-19 between January 1 and 5, 2021, were studied.
69.47% were aged 50 years or older, 53.69% were males, and 21.58% had serious comorbidities.
Vaccinated individuals had a lower risk of ICU admission and mortality compared to unvaccinated individuals.
The number of SARS-CoV-2 vaccine doses received demonstrated an inverse dose-response association with the COVID-19-related ICU admission and death among vaccinated subjects.
No substantial variation in risk for infection with SARS-CoV-2 VOCs was observed among vaccinated and unvaccinated participants.
