Title: Early signs suggest race matters when it comes to COVID-19. So why isn't Canada collecting race-based data?
Publisher: CBC.ca
Posted: Today
The article highlights the importance of collecting race-based data to understand the impact of COVID-19 on vulnerable communities.

Health-care advocates and professionals criticize this approach for not acknowledging existing health disparities and not allowing targeted interventions in vulnerable communities.
The article mentions that Alberta's chief public health officer has committed to exploring race-based data collection.

Title: Ontario scaled back comprehensive, annual inspections of nursing homes to only a handful last year
Publisher: CBC.ca
Posted: 3 days ago
Key values:
Only 9 out of 626 long-term care homes received a comprehensive resident quality inspection (RQI) last year.
RQIs are more rigorous and unannounced, focusing on systemic issues rather than specific complaints or incidents.
The number of RQIs has decreased significantly since 2017, from about 85% to just over half in 2018 and 9 in 2020.
Long-term care homes that have had multiple COVID-19 deaths were not among those that received an RQI last year.
