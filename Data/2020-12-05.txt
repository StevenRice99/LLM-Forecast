Article 3 of 5
Title: COVID-19 caused surge in demand for food banks, which was already on the rise: report
Publisher: CTV News
Posted: 5 days ago
Food bank use surged during the pandemic, with a 5% increase in users from April 2019 to March 2020.
First-time users increased significantly in the first four months of the pandemic (March-June 2020).
Government intervention and community initiatives helped reduce demand later in the pandemic.
Key drivers of continued growth in food bank use are inadequate social supports, precarious employment, and lack of affordable housing.
Food banks saw an increase in employed adults using their services, with a 44% increase over four years.
