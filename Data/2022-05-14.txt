The references provided are a mix of academic articles, government reports, and data analyses related to the COVID-19 pandemic's impact on various populations and communities in Canada and globally. Here's a brief summary of each reference:

2. CIHI (2020): The Canadian Institute for Health Information report compares Canada's long-term care sector experience with other countries during the pandemic.
3. Clark et al. (2020): This study examines the disproportionate impact of COVID-19 on immigrant communities in the United States, highlighting the need for targeted public health responses.
4. Clark (2021): The report discusses the effects of the pandemic on nursing and residential care facilities in Canada.
5. Edward (2021): This analysis explores COVID-19 deaths among immigrants in Canada during the early months of the pandemic.
6. Hawkins et al. (2020): The study investigates the relationship between socioeconomic status and COVID-19-related cases and fatalities, finding a significant association.
7. Ke & Zhenghao (2020): This article examines the link between population density and the spread rate of SARS-CoV-2, suggesting that higher population density is associated with increased transmission rates.
8. Martins-Filho (2021): The study explores the relationship between population density and COVID-19 incidence and mortality estimates at a country-level analysis.
9. Miller et al. (2021): This analysis estimates mortality increases during the pandemic by socioeconomic status, race, and ethnicity in the United States.
10. O'Brien et al. (2020): The study investigates the incidence, clinical features, and outcomes of COVID-19 in Canada, including the impact of sex and age on disease severity.
11. PHAC (2018 & 2020): These reports from the Public Health Agency of Canada discuss key health inequalities in Canada and social inequalities in COVID-19 mortality by area- and individual-level characteristics.
12. Spotswood et al. (2021): The study examines the relationship between nature inequity and higher COVID-19 case rates in less-green neighborhoods in the United States.
13. Statistics Canada (2019): This user guide provides information on the Canadian Index of Multiple Deprivation, a tool for measuring socioeconomic status.
14. Subedi et al. (2020): The analysis explores COVID-19 mortality rates in Canada's ethnocultural neighborhoods.
15. Wadhera et al. (2020): This study examines variation in COVID-19 hospitalizations and deaths across New York City Boroughs.
16. Yanez et al. (2020): The study investigates the risk of COVID-19 mortality for older men and women.
These references provide valuable insights into various aspects of the pandemic's impact on different populations, including socioeconomic status, ethnicity, age, sex, and geographic location.
