Article 8 of 9
Title: What you need to know about COVID-19 in Ottawa on Tuesday, Feb. 8
Publisher: CBC.ca
Posted: 4 days ago
Here are the key points from the article:
Vaccination 
Children aged 5-11 should receive at least two doses, with a minimum interval of eight weeks between doses for optimal protection.
Some health authorities allow parents to request a shorter interval.
A third dose can be given after contracting COVID-19, but only when the person is feeling recovered.
Vaccine Availability 
Over 5 million vaccine doses have been administered in the Ottawa-Gatineau region (2.3 million residents).
Eligible individuals can book appointments online or by phone at 1-833-943-3900.
Local health units offer flexibility, including child-only clinics and limited walk-ins.
Testing 
PCR tests are only available for high-risk settings in eastern Ontario due to Omicron demand.
Rapid COVID-19 tests are available in some child-care settings when risk is high.
Travelers who need a test can pay for one locally.
In western Quebec, PCR tests are reserved for high-risk settings, and rapid tests are available in all daycares, preschools, and elementary schools.
First Nations, Inuit, and Métis Communities 
First Nations, Inuit, and Métis people or those traveling to work in a remote Indigenous community can access testing.
Specific communities have their own COVID-19 test and vaccine information online or by phone:
+ Akausivik Inuit Family Health Team (Ottawa): 613-740-0999
+ Akwesasne: 613-575-2341
+ Kitigan Zibi Anishinabeg: 819-449-8085
+ Pikwàkanagàn: 613-625-1175
