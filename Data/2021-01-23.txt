Article 4 of 5
Title: COVID-19 and Ontario’s Long-Term Care Homes
Publisher: Ontario COVID-19 Science Advisory Table
Posted: 3 days ago
The text discusses strategies for improving the response to COVID-19 in Long-Term Care (LTC) homes in Ontario, Canada. The main points are:
1. Prioritize vaccination: Prioritize vaccination of LTC residents, workers, and essential family caregivers to reduce COVID-19 cases and deaths.
2. Implement effective communication: Implement strategies to expedite administration and improve vaccine acceptance among LTC staff, including education, peer champions, and addressing concerns about the vaccine development process.
3. Optimize data collection: Continue optimizing data on LTC homes for the duration of the pandemic, including resuming expedited reviews of COVID-19 deaths and collecting timely and complete vaccination rates.
4. Enhance resident assessment: Implement the interRAI LTCF, a next-generation assessment tool, to better understand the functional and psychosocial status and care needs of LTC residents.
5. Collect data on essential caregivers and visitors: Collect data on the frequency and nature of visits by essential family caregivers and visitors to understand the risks they may carry with respect to SARS-CoV-2 transmission.
6. Enhance staffing data collection: Enhance data on staffing levels, hours of care provided, and staff infection rates to support community-tailored approaches focused on LTC home workers.
The text highlights the importance of these strategies in preventing COVID-19 outbreaks and deaths within LTC homes during both the pre- and post-COVID-19 vaccine era.
