Article 1 of 8
Title: What's the difference between COVID-19, RSV and influenza?
Publisher: CTV News
Posted: 2 days ago
RSV symptoms include runny nose, sneezing, coughing, sore throat, fatigue and fever, with children and immunocompromised individuals at the highest risk of hospitalization. 
Influenza symptoms typically appear between one and four days after exposure, usually in the form of a fever, coughing and muscle aches or pain.
COVID-19 symptoms include sore throat, runny nose, sneezing, a new or worsening cough, shortness of breath, fever, chills, fatigue, muscle aches, loss of smell or taste, headache, abdominal pain, diarrhea and vomiting.

Title: Triple the usual number of kids are coming to Ontario ERs with respiratory illnesses. Here's why
Publisher: CBC News
Posted: 2 days ago
Respiratory illnesses among children are surging in Ontario, with ER visits and admissions at far higher rates than normal for this time of year.
The number of kids aged 5-17 visiting ERs for respiratory complaints over the past week was more than triple the seasonal average (2,160 vs. 800).
Hospitals across Ontario are admitting an unusually high number of children for respiratory illnesses (2.5 times the pre-pandemic average).
Medical experts link the surge in illnesses among children to the withdrawal of preventive public health measures such as mask-wearing that had been in place since the start of the COVID-19 pandemic.
The trend is happening at comparable rates across all regions of Ontario, putting hospitals under pressure and affecting hospital operations.

Publisher: CBC News

The article discusses the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic in Canada and its impact on hospitals and healthcare systems. Despite vaccination efforts, hospitalizations due to COVID-19 are rising, particularly among vulnerable populations such as seniors.
Physicians note that patients admitted to hospital for other ailments often test positive for COVID-19, while others catch the virus during their stays. This highlights the challenges of managing hospital-acquired outbreaks and the need for continued vigilance in infection control measures.
The article also emphasizes the importance of taking precautions to prevent the spread of COVID-19, particularly among seniors and those with compromised immune systems. Physicians recommend that seniors consider getting vaccinated against COVID-19, influenza, and pneumonia, as well as wearing masks and practicing good hygiene.
The article features a family who lost their 89-year-old mother, Josephine Moschitto, to COVID-19 in October. Her children reflect on the challenges of managing the virus's impact on vulnerable populations and the importance of taking precautions to prevent its spread.
Key points from the article include:
Hospitalizations due to COVID-19 are rising in Canada, particularly among seniors.

Hospital-acquired outbreaks continue to occur, contributing to the rise in hospitalizations.
