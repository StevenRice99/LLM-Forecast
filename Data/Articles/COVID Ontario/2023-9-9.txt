Molecular Tests (PCR and NAATs): These highly accurate tests detect the virus's genetic material. Antigen Tests (Rapid Tests): These tests detect viral proteins and can be completed at home, providing results within minutes. Antibody Tests: These tests do not diagnose a current infection but detect antibodies in the blood produced in response to SARS-CoV-2. They are not currently recommended for checking immunity. Types of Samples: Swab Samples: Collected from the nose or throat, including anterior nares (nasal), mid-turbinate, nasopharyngeal, and oropharyngeal swabs. Saliva Samples: Collected by spitting into a tube, offering an alternative to nose or throat swabs. Blood Samples: Used exclusively for antibody testing and collected through finger sticks or venous blood draws. Authorized Uses and Test Locations: Diagnostic tests can be performed at laboratories, testing sites, medical offices, or at home. At-home tests offer convenience but may have expiration dates and generally provide less accuracy than laboratory-based molecular tests. Repeat testing improves accuracy for at-home antigen tests. Community testing sites and authorized at-home tests are available for those who need COVID-19 diagnostic testing.
