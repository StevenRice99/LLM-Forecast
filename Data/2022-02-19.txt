Article 3 of 8
Title: What you need to know about COVID-19 in Ottawa on Thursday, Feb. 17
Publisher: CBC.ca
Posted: 2 days ago
This article provides information on the current COVID-19 situation in Canada, specifically in Ontario and Quebec. Here are some key points:
Travel to Canada 
Fully vaccinated Canadians can enter Canada without a PCR test if they're asymptomatic and test negative.
As of February 28, fully vaccinated Canadians can take an authorized rapid test instead of a PCR test.
US Travel Requirements 
The US requires all adults crossing a land, air, or water border to be fully vaccinated.
People flying to the US need proof of a negative COVID-19 test within one day of departure.
Vaccines 
Five COVID-19 vaccines have been deemed safe and approved in Canada, with some age restrictions.
Local health authorities recommend doses for kids aged 5-11 be given at least eight weeks apart for optimal protection.
Guidance varies on when to get a third dose after contracting COVID-19.
Testing 
In eastern Ontario:
+ Only high-risk people can get a laboratory-checked PCR test due to Omicron demand.
+ Rapid and take-home tests are available for the general public at participating stores and in some child-care settings.
In western Quebec:
+ PCR tests will be reserved for high-risk settings such as hospitals, long-term care homes, detention centers, and homeless shelters.
+ Rapid COVID-19 tests are available in all Quebec daycares, preschools, and elementary schools.
First Nations, Inuit, and Métis 
First Nations, Inuit, and Métis people can get a test in both Ontario and Quebec.
There are specific resources for Indigenous communities, including testing and vaccine information online or by phone.
Symptoms and Treatment 
COVID-19 symptoms range from a cold-like illness to severe lung infection.
Health Canada has approved Pfizer's COVID-19 prescription treatment Paxlovid.
If you have severe symptoms, call 911.
